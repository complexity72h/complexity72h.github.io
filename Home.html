COMPLEXITY 72H
ABOUT

We are glad to announce the fifth edition of the Complexity72h workshop, which will take place in Madrid, Spain, in June 2024.

Complexity72h is an interdisciplinary workshop for young researchers in complex systems. Participants form teams and carry out projects in a three days' time, i.e. 72 hours. The goal of each team is to upload on the arXiv (or similar repositories) a report of their work by the end of the event. The editions of 2018 and 2019 were a success: 11 out of 11 projects have become arXiv preprints and new collaborations have been born. The 2020 edition was canceled due to the pandemic. But we were back for the 2023 edition, which took place in Palma (Mallorca, Spain) on June 26-30. 

CALL FOR TUTORS!

Complexity72h is an interdisciplinary workshop for young researchers in complex systems. Participants form teams and carry out projects in a three days' time, i.e. 72 hours. The goal of each team is to upload on the arXiv (or similar repositories) a report of their work by the end of the event. The editions of 2018, 2019 and 2023 were a success: 11 out of 11 projects have become arXiv preprints and new collaborations have been born. We are now back for a 2024 edition, which will take place in Madrid (Spain) in June. And we need you. Would you like to be a tutor at the next edition of Complexity72h? Then you should apply to the call for tutors!.

Accommodation and meals for the five days of the workshop will be covered for accepted tutors. Travel expenses may be partially or fully covered depending on the budget.

Tutors will be experienced researchers proposing a project to be developed in just 72 hours by a group of about six workshop participants.

We cannot guarantee that all tutors will be accepted for the workshop and all the projects will be starting. This will depend on the number of workshop applicants choosing each project. The final list of accepted tutors and projects will be made public by the end of March.

You will need to send to the address complexity72h [at] gmail [dot] com an email with subject  "TUTOR APPLICATION - ", containing the following attachments:

Your CV (2 pages max);

A motivation letter (1 or 2 pages);

The title of the project;

An abstract of the project you wish to propose. At this stage, your project does not need to be fully formed or final, but you should be able to give us a clear idea of what you will be working on. Also, please list the data that you will use, and remember that it is up to you to provide them to the participants. If your project is selected this abstract will appear online (you will have the chance to edit it before this happens).
